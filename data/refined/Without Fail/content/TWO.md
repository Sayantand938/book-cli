Froelich watched the people's faces. She looked around the edges of the area. She watched the crowd, trying hard to feel if anything changed in how they were acting as a group. She wanted to know if they were tense, worried, or suddenly scared. But she didn't see anything like that. She also didn't see any sign of Reacher.

Armstrong stayed thirty minutes longer than he planned. The weak autumn sun made the field look golden, there was no wind, and he was enjoying himself. He didn't have any plans for the evening except a quiet dinner with important people from the state government. So, his wife was taken home, and his security team guided him back to the cars. They drove him north into the city of Bismarck. There was a hotel next to the restaurant, and Froelich had booked rooms there for them to rest before the meal. Armstrong slept for an hour, then took a shower and got dressed. The dinner was going well when his top assistant received a phone call.

The current President and Vice President were officially inviting the new President and Vice President to a one-day meeting to help with the change of power. It would be at the Naval Support Facility in Thurmont, starting early the next morning. This was a normal invitation, because they needed to talk about important things. It was sent in the usual way, at the last minute and in a bossy tone. This was because the people leaving office wanted to feel powerful one last time. But Froelich was happy because the Naval Support Facility in Thurmont is also known as Camp David. It's one of the safest places in the world, hidden in the Maryland mountains. She decided they should all fly back to Andrews Air Force Base right away and take Marine helicopters straight to Camp David. If they spent all night and all day there, she could relax completely for twenty-four hours. But late on Sunday morning, a navy worker found her having breakfast in the dining hall. He plugged a telephone into a wall socket near her chair. Nobody uses wireless or cell phones at Camp David because they can be easily listened to.

"Call transferred from your main office, ma'am," the worker said.

There was silence for a moment, and then a voice spoke. "We should meet," Reacher said.

"Why?"

"I can't tell you on the phone."

"Where have you been?"

"Here and there."

"Where are you now?"

"In the hotel room you used for the party on Thursday."

"Do you have something important for me?"

"A final answer."

"Already? It's only been five days. You said it would take ten."

"Five days was enough."

Froelich covered the phone with her hand. "What's the final answer?" She then held her breath, waiting.

"It can't be done," Reacher said.

She let out her breath and smiled. "I told you so."

"No, what you're trying to do can't be done. You need to talk to me right away. You should come here now."
